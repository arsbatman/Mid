Clean Code: A Handbook of Agile Software Craftsmanship by Robert C. Martin

Poorly written code can bring a project to its knees, which is why developing great code is so important! In Clean Code, “Uncle Bob” Martin shares tips and examples on how to create better code. The book dives into the principles and best practices of writing clean code, and also presents increasingly challenging case studies presented that challenges readers to think about what’s right with the code, and what’s wrong with it. While examples in Clean Code are given in Java, but is applicable to nearly all programming languages.
TIP: Read Clean Code after getting through Code Complete since it deals with some of the same topics but at a higher level.

Suddenly, military helicopters arrive at the scene of the accident and take both of them with them. Meanwhile, experiments are being carried out on Tetsuo, turning him into the latest type of parapsychological weapon, the best example of which was at one time a mysterious boy named Akira.

Moby Dick by Herman Melville

Herman Melville (1819–1891) was an American writer and sailor, in whose work and destiny the experience of a traveler and the artist's mythopoetic worldview were surprisingly organically melted. The realization of the magnitude of Melville's talent did not come immediately, and only a quarter of a century after the death of the writer began to see the outlines of the enormous contribution he made to the treasury of world literature. The central work of Melville - the grandiose "Moby Dick" - became one of the peaks of American literature, finally depriving America of the status of "cultural desert". This edition includes, in addition to "Moby Dick", a number of the most significant stories and stories, giving an idea of ​​the versatility of Herman Melville's talent and his writing skills.

War and Peace by Leo Tolstoy

LN Tolstoy's life, creative path, ideological searches captivate the minds of our contemporaries, who are trying to understand the very essence of the writer's work and his teachings. "War and Peace" is the pinnacle of LN Tolstoy's work. Tolstoy, like no other work of the writer, reflects the depth of his attitude and philosophy. This book is from the category of eternal ones, because it is about everything - about life and death, about love and honor, about courage and heroism, about glory and heroism, about war and peace. For the third century, the world's most famous novel by a genius writer has made readers empathize with the heroes of the work. The novel is about the Russian soul, about the Russian way of life, eternal questions that each person has to decide on their own. All the ups and downs of the heroes taking place against the background of historical events that took place in the early nineteenth century with Russia, against the background of the bloody events of the war of 1812, acquire a capacious philosophical meaning. The novel is still in the first place in all world literature in terms of depth and coverage of events.

Heart of Darkness by Joseph Conrad

The story is told from the perspective of the main character, sailor Marlowe, who recalls his trip to Central Africa. On the instructions of the "Company" (the text is not named, but apparently, the Belgian company of the Free Congo is implied), he must arrive at a remote station to take with him one of the company's agents named Kurtz, who collects ivory. The main part of the book is occupied by a first-person story of Marlowe about his journey along a tropical river, among territories completely unknown to Europeans. His story is full of biased, but therefore no less creepy, details of both the life of the aborigines and the orders imposed in a distant colony. However, most of all, the main character terrifies Kurtz as the embodiment of primitive instincts that are hidden in the depths of every person's soul and which, if given free rein, will completely subjugate him.
